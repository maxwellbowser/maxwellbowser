About Me

I'm Ryan Bowser, a senior at the University of Arizona, diving into the intricate world of cardiac protein interactions within the Gregorio lab. Beyond my academic pursuits, I find joy in hitting the pavement for runs, crafting artisanal sourdough bread, and immersing myself in the pages of captivating books.

Education & Skills

Currently on an accelerated path toward an M.S. in Molecular & Cellular Biology, expected to graduate with honors in both my B.S. and M.S. by 2025. I possess a keen eye for detail, stellar time management, and a robust skill set spanning Python programming, embryo microinjections, sterile techniques, and PCR.

Awards & Recognition

Throughout my academic journey, I've been honored with distinctions like the Highest Academic Distinction and the Bravo! Award at the University of Arizona, acknowledging my dedication and contributions.

Relevant Experience

My research tenure in the Gregorio lab involves unraveling the complex interactions of cardiac actin and regulatory proteins. Notably, I've spearheaded the development of an automated data analysis pipeline, revolutionizing our data processing methods and significantly cutting costs.

Outreach & Volunteering

I've taken my passion beyond the lab, presenting my research at the 34th Annual UBRP Conference and engaging in impactful volunteer work, from serving meals to migrant communities to inspiring high school students through presentations on biological research.

Past Endeavors

From conducting independent research on endophytes to exploring communication signals in plateau lizards, my journey has been diverse. I've honed my skills in phenotype analysis, imaging techniques, and experimental design across various research environments.
